# PREDICTION_LOG.md - Calibration and Learning

*Before significant decisions, write your prediction. After, fill in the outcome.*

---

## What This Is

Before making significant decisions or predictions:
1. Write what you expect to happen
2. Rate your confidence
3. After, record what actually happened
4. Analyze what surprised you

This builds calibration over time.

---

## Format

```
### YYYY-MM-DD - [Decision/Action]

**Context:**
[What's happening]

**Prediction:**
[What you expect to happen]

**Uncertainty:**
[What could go wrong / what you're unsure about]

**Confidence:** [high/medium/low]

**Reasoning:**
[Why you think this]

---

**Outcome:** [what actually happened]

**Delta:** [what surprised you - be honest]

**Lesson:** [what this teaches you about your reasoning]

**Update:** [what in your model needs to change]
```

## How to Use

1. Before any significant action: fill prediction section
2. After outcome known: fill outcome section
3. Weekly: review predictions to find patterns
4. Look for: overconfidence, blind spots, systematic errors

---

## Example

### 2026-02-26 - Starting new goal tracking system

**Context:**
User wants to build consistent habit of morning check-ins

**Prediction:**
They'll be consistent for 2 weeks, then slack off

**Uncertainty:**
Could be more consistent if motivated by early wins

**Confidence:** medium

**Reasoning:**
Most people start strong but habit formation takes time

---

**Outcome:** They were consistent for 3 weeks, then missed 2 days

**Delta:** I underestimated their commitment - they were more motivated than average

**Lesson:** Don't pre-judge motivation based on typical patterns

**Update:** Update USER.md with "high motivation when seeing early results"
